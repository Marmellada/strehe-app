# STREHE Visual Production Plan

## Purpose

This document defines the visual assets needed for the STREHE public website and how they should be produced.

The goal is not to gather random pretty images.

The goal is to build a small, coherent visual system that supports:

- diaspora-family trust
- local credibility
- visible care
- clear conversion

---

## 1. Production principle

Every visual should answer at least one of these questions:

- Does this make the service feel more real?
- Does this make the apartment feel actively cared for?
- Does this make the company feel more trustworthy?
- Does this make the site feel more respectful and polished?

If the visual only makes the site look more expensive, stylish, or luxurious, it is not enough.

---

## 2. Visual categories we need

We should produce visuals in four categories.

### Category A: Hero images

Purpose:

- establish emotional tone
- create trust fast
- avoid generic SaaS or real-estate-listing energy

These should feel:

- warm
- calm
- grounded
- cared for

### Category B: Service-support images

Purpose:

- show active care
- make the service easy to imagine
- support sections like services, process, and trust

These should show:

- apartment checks
- preparation
- issue follow-up
- communication/reporting

### Category C: Trust-detail images

Purpose:

- support trust sections with small proof-oriented moments
- show signs of responsibility rather than generic beauty

These should show:

- notes or checklist moments
- keys/access handled carefully
- phone/report updates
- preparation details

### Category D: Fallback brand surfaces

Purpose:

- support cards, section dividers, or empty visual areas if needed
- create consistent mood without adding visual noise

These should be:

- subtle
- atmospheric
- secondary

---

## 3. Launch asset list

For the first high-quality version of the site, we should aim for this minimum set:

1. Homepage hero image
2. Secondary homepage image for “Why This Matters” or “What STREHE Handles”
3. Services page lead image
4. How It Works page lead image
5. About page lead image
6. Contact page image
7. Two small trust-detail images

That is enough to make the site feel intentional without overbuilding.

---

## 4. Asset-by-asset direction

## Asset 1: Homepage hero image

### Job

- make the service feel calm, serious, and trustworthy
- show an apartment that feels cared for
- avoid empty-luxury real-estate energy

### Best direction

A warm, well-kept apartment interior in Prishtina/Fushë Kosovë spirit, with subtle signs that someone responsible has recently checked or prepared the space.

### Important signals

- clean but not sterile
- lived-in but orderly
- warm natural light
- premium but grounded
- hints of preparation or care

### Avoid

- ultra-luxury penthouse feel
- cold architectural show-home feel
- hotel-room feel
- interior-design-magazine perfection

---

## Asset 2: Homepage trust/support image

### Job

- support the “problem” or “services” story
- show active care rather than passive beauty

### Best direction

A responsible local person checking the apartment carefully, using a checklist or phone, in a calm and respectful way.

### Important signals

- no staged smiling-at-camera energy
- attention to detail
- home care, not repair chaos
- professionalism without uniformed corporate stiffness

---

## Asset 3: Services page lead image

### Job

- make the service categories feel real
- help visitors picture what happens

### Best direction

A scene suggesting practical apartment readiness: checking windows, preparing a room, airing the space, or making the apartment ready before arrival.

---

## Asset 4: How It Works page lead image

### Job

- support the process story
- reduce uncertainty

### Best direction

A subtle reporting/communication moment: phone in hand, checklist, photo update, or calm documentation after a visit.

---

## Asset 5: About page lead image

### Job

- humanize the business
- make it feel local and real

### Best direction

A grounded local-care image with emotional warmth, not a founder glamour portrait.

If people are shown, they should feel:

- responsible
- respectful
- natural
- understated

---

## Asset 6: Contact page image

### Job

- make contact feel low-pressure and human
- reinforce that the first step is just a conversation

### Best direction

A calm communication-oriented image: a thoughtful local work moment, phone, notes, or an inviting apartment detail that feels cared for and approachable.

---

## Asset 7-8: Trust-detail images

### Job

- support trust and process sections
- show evidence of care

### Best directions

- keys and access handled carefully
- checklist / notes / update moment
- small maintenance follow-up moment
- final apartment readiness detail

### Temporary rule for key cabinet visuals

We do not need to freeze the exact key cabinet model before using key-handling visuals on the site.

For now, we can use:

- brand-neutral secure key handling
- a simple lockable cabinet
- careful key organization

Later, once the real cabinet is chosen, we can replace those visuals with the exact setup.

---

## 5. Style rules for generated visuals

All generated visuals should follow these rules:

- realistic photography style
- warm natural lighting
- soft premium finish
- grounded interior realism
- no exaggerated luxury styling
- no flashy color grading
- no fake business-stock smiling
- no aggressive cinematic drama
- no generic hotel or Airbnb look

The images should feel like:

- responsible care
- quiet competence
- home respect

---

## 6. Human-presence rule

If a person appears in an image, they should be shown as:

- calm
- respectful
- attentive
- practical

They should not look like:

- influencers
- real-estate salespeople
- lifestyle models
- corporate stock-photo actors

The human role in the image is to suggest:

- accountability
- care
- presence

not performance.

---

## 7. Location and cultural feel

The visuals do not need to shout “Kosovo” in a cliché way.

But they should feel plausible for:

- modern apartments in Prishtina
- apartments in Fushë Kosovë
- diaspora family ownership

The tone should feel:

- Balkan-European
- urban
- practical
- cared for

without becoming stereotyped.

---

## 8. Prompt strategy

When generating visuals, prompts should emphasize:

- apartment care
- responsible presence
- natural light
- calm premium realism
- diaspora-family trust

Prompts should avoid vague words like:

- luxury
- elegant
- premium

unless paired with concrete grounding details.

Better prompt ingredients:

- well-kept apartment interior
- natural light
- subtle signs of active care
- checklist or phone update
- respectful local service moment
- realistic photography
- no staged stock-photo expression

---

## 9. First prompt set to generate

We should start with these three image directions first:

### Prompt A: homepage hero

Realistic photography of a warm, well-kept apartment interior in a modern Balkan-European city, natural daylight, calm premium but grounded atmosphere, subtle signs of active care and readiness, lived-in but orderly, no hotel feel, no luxury real-estate glamour, no people looking at camera, diaspora-family trust, respectful polished home-care mood

### Prompt B: apartment check moment

Realistic photography of a responsible local person quietly checking an apartment interior, holding a phone or checklist, natural light, calm and professional, no staged smile, no corporate uniform, no repair chaos, the apartment feels clean, lived-in, and respectfully maintained, trust-focused home-care service mood

### Prompt C: reporting / follow-up moment

Realistic photography of a calm apartment-care reporting moment, phone in hand, notes or checklist visible, tidy apartment background, natural warm light, grounded and trustworthy, no stock-photo acting, no flashy luxury styling, clear feeling of structured follow-up and responsible care

---

## 10. Recommended production order

1. homepage hero image
2. apartment-check image
3. reporting/follow-up image
4. services lead image
5. about image
6. contact image
7. trust-detail images

---

## 11. Working rule for acceptance

We should accept an image only if it passes this test:

1. Does it look credible?
2. Does it support trust?
3. Does it show care, not just beauty?
4. Would it feel respectable to share with family?
5. Does it avoid looking like a listing site, hotel brand, or startup ad?

If not, we regenerate.
