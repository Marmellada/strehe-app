# STREHË Respondent Testing Plan

## Purpose

This document defines how to test the STREHË idea, packages, pricing, and trust story with real respondents before locking the offer.

The goal is not to get compliments.

The goal is to find:

- what people understand
- what they distrust
- what feels expensive
- what feels unclear
- what would make them contact STREHË

---

## 1. Who to test with

Start with:

- 10 to 20 respondents

Best respondents:

- Kosovo diaspora living in Germany, Switzerland, Austria, UK, USA, or broader Europe
- people who own an apartment in Prishtina or Fushë Kosovë
- people whose family owns an apartment there
- spouses or family members who influence household decisions

Avoid testing only with:

- people who want to be nice
- people with no property connection
- people who cannot imagine paying for local care

---

## 2. Respondent groups

Try to include a mix.

| Group | Why they matter |
| --- | --- |
| Apartment owner abroad | Primary buyer |
| Spouse / partner | Trust and comfort decision-maker |
| Family member managing property informally | Shows current alternative |
| Diaspora person planning to buy/keep property | Future buyer insight |
| Local person in Kosovo | Reality check for delivery expectations |

---

## 3. What to show respondents

Do not start with a long explanation.

Show a simple version:

1. one-sentence explanation of STREHË
2. the three package table
3. what is included / not included
4. one sample visit report
5. how contact would happen

### One-sentence explanation

Use something like:

`STREHË checks and cares for apartments in Prishtina and Fushë Kosovë for owners who live abroad, with regular visits, clear updates, and local follow-up when needed.`

---

## 4. Package table to show

| Package | Draft price | Visits | Best for |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Essential Check | `€69/month` | 1/month | Basic reassurance |
| Care Plus | `€109/month` | 2/month | Most owners abroad |
| Arrival Ready | `€159/month` | 2/month + arrival support | Owners returning or needing more active care |

Also show:

- repairs are not included
- technician costs are paid separately
- cleaning is separate unless arranged
- extra visits cost extra
- no 24/7 emergency promise at launch

---

## 5. Interview rules

Do not ask:

- “Do you like it?”
- “Would you use this?”

These questions create polite answers.

Ask instead:

- “What would stop you?”
- “What sounds unclear?”
- “What would you need to trust this?”
- “Which package would you actually consider?”
- “What price starts feeling too much?”

---

## Section C: trust test

Ask:

1. What would you need to trust a company with apartment access?
2. What would make you hesitate?
3. Would photo updates help?

## Section E: pricing reaction

Ask directly but calmly.

For each package, ask:

1. Does this feel cheap, fair, expensive, or too expensive?
2. At what price would this start feeling too expensive?
3. What would need to be included for this price to feel fair?

## 7. Sample visit report to show

Use a simple example.

### Example

**Visit date:** May 15, 2026

**Apartment status:** Normal. No urgent issue noticed.

**Checked:**

- entrance/access
- living area
- kitchen visible condition
- bathroom visible condition
- windows/airing
- visible water/electricity concerns

**Notes:**

- apartment was clean and orderly
- no visible leak or damage
- living room aired during visit

**Photos:**

- living area
- kitchen
- bathroom
- balcony/window area

**Next action:**

- no action needed

This helps respondents understand what “checked” means.

## 8. What to listen for

The most important signals are:

| Signal | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| “I would need to trust the person first” | About/founder/contract story matters |
| “My family can do this” | Need stronger comparison against informal help |
| “€109 is too high” | Core package may need more value or lower entry |
| “I would try it once” | Trial visit may be useful |
| “I need this before summer visits” | Arrival-ready timing is a strong use case |
| “Who keeps the keys?” | Key/access system must be shown clearly |
| “Does this include cleaning?” | Boundaries need to be clearer |

---

## 9. Result scoring

After each respondent, score:

| Category | Score |
| --- | --- |
| Problem fit | 1-5 |
| Trust comfort | 1-5 |
| Package clarity | 1-5 |
| Price acceptance | 1-5 |
| Likelihood to contact | 1-5 |

### Meaning

- 1 = very weak
- 3 = mixed
- 5 = strong

Do not average everything too early.

Look for repeated objections.

## 11. Decision rules after 10-20 tests

After testing, decide:

### Keep

If respondents understand it quickly and react positively.

### Change

If multiple respondents are confused by the same thing.

### Remove

If people consistently misunderstand it or it creates expectations STREHË cannot meet.

### Add

If the same missing trust point appears repeatedly.

---

## 12. What results would validate the offer?

The offer is promising if:

- at least half say the problem is real
- most understand the core package
- `Care Plus` feels like the natural middle option
- people ask about trust/access more than they reject the concept
- at least a few would consider messaging or trying a first visit

The offer needs revision if:

- people think it is only cleaning
- people think family can fully replace it
- pricing feels high before value is understood
- people cannot explain the package back clearly
- trust concerns are too strong and unanswered

---

## 13. Recommended first test size

Do not overbuild the survey.

Start with:

- 5 informal interviews

Then adjust the script.

Then do:

- 10 to 15 more structured interviews

This gives enough signal without waiting forever.

---

## 14. Working summary

The test should answer:

- do diaspora owners recognize the problem?
- do they understand the packages?
- do the prices feel believable?
- what trust proof do they need?
- what would make them actually contact STREHË?

The purpose is not validation theater.

The purpose is to make the offer clearer, safer, and more sellable before launch.
