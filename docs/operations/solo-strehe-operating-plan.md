# Solo STREHË Operating Plan

## Purpose

This document defines the first operating model for STREHË if the business starts with one founder only.

The goal is not to simulate a large company.

The goal is to run a small, reliable, trustworthy service that can grow later.

---

## 1. Operating principle

Solo STREHË should optimize for:

- reliability
- clarity
- manageable workload
- repeatable process
- trust

It should not optimize for:

- looking bigger than it is
- taking every opportunity
- covering too much territory
- offering every possible service

---

## 2. Launch scope

### Geography

At launch:

- Prishtina
- Fushë Kosovë

### Property type

At launch:

- apartments only

### Customer type

At launch:

- diaspora apartment owners living abroad

### Not included yet

- houses
- nationwide support
- full renovation project management
- open-ended concierge services
- 24/7 emergency promises

---

## 3. Core services for solo v1

Solo STREHË should begin with only a small set of clear services.

## Service 1: Regular apartment checks

Includes:

- scheduled visits
- visible-condition checks
- readiness checks
- short updates with photos

This is the operational core.

## Service 2: Arrival-ready preparation

Includes:

- pre-arrival visit
- airing rooms if needed
- making sure the apartment feels ready before return
- basic coordination of small readiness details

## Service 3: Issue reporting and follow-up

Includes:

- noticing issues early
- documenting clearly
- informing the owner
- following up locally on small fixes or service needs

## Service 4: Limited practical owner support

Includes:

- a narrow amount of apartment-related local help
- simple tasks that fit the service model

Rule:

If a request creates chaos, depends on too many unknowns, or breaks routine, it should probably not be part of v1.

---

## 4. What solo STREHË should not offer yet

To protect service quality, solo STREHË should avoid:

- broad “anything you need” promises
- custom one-off errand chaos
- large renovation management
- too many emergency-style expectations
- unclear availability promises
- service commitments that depend on helpers who are not under control

The founder should stay disciplined.

Saying no early is part of protecting the business.

---

## 5. First capacity target

The first goal is not scale.

The first goal is proof of reliable delivery.

### Recommended early capacity

Start with:

- 3 to 8 apartments

This leaves room for:

- learning the real workload
- building routines
- creating reporting standards
- improving customer communication
- handling unexpected issues without panic

### Capacity rule

Only grow when the process feels calm and repeatable.

If the founder feels reactive all the time, growth is happening too early.

---

## 6. Weekly operating rhythm

The founder needs a simple, repeatable weekly rhythm.

### Suggested weekly structure

#### Monday

- review current apartments and notes
- respond to client messages
- plan visits for the week
- flag any open issues or follow-ups

#### Tuesday to Thursday

- perform scheduled apartment visits
- send updates after visits
- handle small follow-up coordination

#### Friday

- catch up on unresolved items
- complete billing/admin tasks
- review the week
- prepare next week’s priorities

### Daily rule

Each day should contain only a manageable mix of:

- visits
- communication
- follow-up
- admin

Do not stack too many different kinds of work into one day without structure.

---

## 7. Standard visit workflow

Every apartment visit should follow a standard rhythm.

### Before the visit

- confirm schedule
- check prior notes
- know what to look for

### During the visit

- check visible condition
- check basic readiness
- note anything unusual
- take clear photos where needed

### After the visit

- send short clear update
- attach photos if relevant
- log anything that needs follow-up

### Visit rule

The client should feel:

- someone was there
- someone paid attention
- someone will follow through if needed

---

## 8. Reporting standard

The update format should stay simple and repeatable.

### Basic report structure

1. date of visit
2. short status summary
3. anything normal/confirmed
4. any issue noticed
5. next action if needed

### Tone of reporting

Reports should be:

- calm
- clear
- specific
- short enough to read easily

Not:

- dramatic
- vague
- overloaded with unnecessary detail

---

## 9. Key and access handling

Even without a final office or final cabinet choice, access handling needs rules from day one.

### Immediate requirements

- key inventory
- clear naming system
- secure storage
- consistent handling process

### Temporary solo rule

If the final key cabinet is not chosen yet:

- use a secure temporary storage solution
- keep written tracking
- do not let the system stay informal for long

### Permanent goal

Move toward:

- a lockable key management system
- clearly tracked access
- low chance of mix-ups

Trust depends heavily on this.

---

## 10. Client communication model

Solo STREHË should communicate in a way that feels:

- personal
- calm
- direct
- structured

### Communication channels

Recommended:

- WhatsApp for quick contact
- email for summaries and formal follow-up
- phone when needed

### Rule

Do not let communication become chaotic.

Clients should not feel ignored, but the founder also should not become permanently on-call.

---

## 11. Billing and admin

Admin should stay simple at launch.

### Needs from the start

- clear service records
- invoice rhythm
- payment tracking
- client contact records

### Billing rhythm

Recommended:

- one consistent billing cadence

For example:

- monthly

The exact pricing structure can evolve later, but the cadence should stay predictable.

---

## 12. Minimum tools needed

Solo STREHË does not need a huge setup to begin.

### Minimum starting tools

- phone
- calendar
- secure key/access method
- transport plan
- reporting format
- invoice/payment method
- client record system
- website
- operational app

This is enough for v1.

---

## 13. Risks in solo mode

Main solo risks:

- overpromising
- taking too many apartments too fast
- unclear service boundaries
- messy communication
- burnout
- reactive work replacing structured work

### Protection strategy

Protect the business through:

- narrow scope
- clear routines
- realistic capacity
- saying no
- disciplined communication

---

## 14. What makes solo STREHË strong

Being solo is not only a weakness.

It can also create strengths:

- one responsible point of contact
- direct accountability
- consistent service standard
- personal trust
- less internal confusion

This should be seen as a launch advantage, not only a limitation.

---

## 15. 30-day operating focus

The first 30 days should focus on:

1. setting up the basic operating system
2. defining the exact service boundaries
3. preparing secure access handling
4. refining the website and messaging
5. being ready for the first small group of clients

The goal is not full scale.

The goal is readiness.

---

## 16. First growth rule

Do not grow because demand exists alone.

Grow when:

- visits feel repeatable
- reporting feels easy
- communication feels manageable
- billing/admin is under control
- no part of the service depends on improvisation every week

If those are not true yet, growth should wait.

---

## 17. Working summary

Solo STREHË v1 should be:

- small
- disciplined
- apartment-only
- geographically narrow
- trust-driven
- operationally clear

The founder does not need to build a full company immediately.

The founder needs to prove one thing first:

`that a calm, reliable, local apartment-care service can be delivered well and consistently by one responsible operator.`
