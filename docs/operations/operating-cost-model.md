# STREHË Operating Cost Model

## Purpose

This document lists the operational costs STREHË should plan for.

It complements:

- `regional-price-benchmark.md`
- `package-pricing-draft.md`
- `year-1-financial-model.md`
- `solo-strehe-operating-plan.md`

The goal is to avoid a common early mistake:

`pricing packages while forgetting the real costs of delivering them.`

---

## 1. Cost categories

STREHË costs should be separated into five groups.

| Cost group | Meaning | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Fixed monthly costs | Costs that exist even with few clients | phone, software, accountant |
| Variable operating costs | Costs that grow with clients/visits | fuel, parking, supplies |
| Staff costs | Cost of operational/admin help | field helper, coordinator |
| Pass-through client costs | Costs the owner should pay separately | repairs, cleaning, technician fees |
| One-time setup costs | Early startup purchases | key cabinet, labels, tools, business setup |

This separation matters because not every cost should be absorbed by STREHË.

## 3. Staff cost model

Staff should be added gradually.

Do not start with full fixed payroll unless the revenue mix supports it.

## Staffing alternatives table

| Stage | Staff model | Suggested monthly cost | When it fits |
| --- | --- | ---: | --- |
| 0-8 clients | Founder only | `€0` | Learning phase |
| 8-15 clients | One part-time field helper | `€300–€500` | Visits start becoming the bottleneck |
| 12-20 clients | Stronger part-time ops helper | `€500–€700` | Reliable weekly support needed |
| 15-25 clients | Ops helper + occasional admin | `€650–€1,000` | Communication/admin begins to pile up |
| 25-35 clients | Field ops + part-time coordinator or second helper | `€1,000–€1,400` | Growth is real but still needs flexibility |
| 30+ clients with strong revenue | Two stronger staff | `€1,400–€1,800+` | Only if average client value supports it |

### Wage recommendation for early STREHË

At 30 clients, if average revenue per client is only around:

- `€100–€110/month`

then combined staff cost should ideally stay around:

- `€900–€1,200/month`

If average revenue per client is closer to:

- `€130–€160/month`

then combined staff cost can more safely move toward:

- `€1,200–€1,800/month`

## 5. Costs that should be passed to the client

These should usually not be absorbed into the monthly package.

| Cost | Who should pay | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Repairs | Client | Plumber, electrician, locksmith, appliance repair |
| Parts/materials | Client | Bulbs, parts, replacement items |
| Deep cleaning | Client | Either direct partner cost or quoted separately |
| Technician fees | Client | STREHË may charge coordination separately |
| Large errands | Client/add-on | Should not become hidden free labor |
| Renovation work | Client | Not included in launch packages |
| Emergency external services | Client | Must be approved/handled clearly |

### Rule

STREHË can coordinate, document, and follow up.

But STREHË should not silently pay for external work from its own margin.

---

## 6. Costs that are dangerous if hidden

These costs can quietly destroy margins if not controlled.

| Hidden cost | Why it is dangerous | Protection |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Extra WhatsApp time | Feels small but accumulates | Define response expectations |
| Unpriced errands | Turns service into concierge work | Use add-ons or refuse |
| Unplanned extra visits | Burns time/fuel | Charge extra visit fee |
| Technician chasing | Can take many calls | Charge coordination fee |
| Waiting time | Kills schedule | Define appointment rules |
| Cleaning coordination | Often bigger than expected | Keep separate or quote |
| Key handling mistakes | High trust/risk cost | Use strict key system |

## 7. Scenario: solo early operation

Assume:

- 5 clients
- average client value: `€89/month`
- no staff
- non-staff monthly costs: `€600`

### Revenue

- `5 x 89 = €445`

### Costs

- staff: `€0`
- non-staff operating costs: `€600`

### Meaning

The very early stage may not cover all costs.

That is acceptable only if treated as startup/proof stage.

## 13. Operational cost rules

## Rule 1: No silent repairs

Repairs, parts, and technician costs must be paid by the client.

## Rule 2: No unlimited communication

WhatsApp is useful, but the service cannot become unlimited emotional support.

## Rule 3: Extra visits cost extra

If it is outside the package rhythm, it should usually be billed.

## Rule 4: Cleaning is separate

Do not include cleaning inside recurring packages unless the margin is clear.

## Rule 5: Office waits

A real office should wait unless it clearly improves operations or trust enough to justify the cost.

## Rule 6: Key handling is not optional

Secure access handling is a core trust cost.

It should be treated as essential infrastructure.

## 14. What this changes in the pricing view

The pricing draft is believable from a market perspective, but the cost model shows:

- low-tier clients alone cannot support staff
- 30 clients is not automatically financially comfortable
- staff must stay flexible until average client value rises
- add-ons and pass-through rules are important
- non-staff operating costs may be higher than the first simple model assumed

### Practical conclusion

The safest Year 1 model is:

- keep Essential Check limited
- make Care Plus the main offer
- protect Arrival Ready from becoming unlimited concierge service
- charge add-ons clearly
- hire flexibly before hiring fixed full-time staff

---

## 15. Working summary

STREHË should plan for:

- monthly non-staff costs of around `€500–€800` in the early solo phase
- monthly non-staff costs of around `€1,200–€1,800` around 30 clients
- staff costs that scale carefully, not automatically
- client-paid repairs, cleaning, materials, and technician fees

The core financial rule is:

`STREHË must not let trust-based service turn into unpriced labor.`
