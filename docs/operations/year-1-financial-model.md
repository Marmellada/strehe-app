# STREHË Year 1 Financial Model

## Purpose

This document provides a simple financial model for STREHË Year 1.

It is not a final accounting document.

It is a planning tool for:

- pricing decisions
- staffing timing
- client-capacity thinking
- founder expectations

The purpose is to understand what the business can realistically support at different client counts.

---

## 1. Planning assumptions

This model uses simple planning assumptions, not exact future facts.

The numbers should be updated later when:

- final service packages are defined
- real client behavior is known
- actual staffing costs become clear
- tax/accounting structure is finalized

### Working assumptions

- launch date: May 1, 2026
- launch geography: Prishtina + Fushë Kosovë
- property type: apartments only
- business model: recurring monthly service
- staffing model: founder + support as needed

---

## 2. Monthly price scenarios

To make planning easier, we use three price levels per active client per month.

### Low

- `€120 / client / month`

### Mid

- `€180 / client / month`

### Strong

- `€250 / client / month`

These are not final package prices.

They are planning levels to test whether the business is healthy at different sizes.

---

## 4. Staff cost scenarios

The real cost of staff depends on:

- full-time vs part-time
- employment structure
- local market rate
- whether the role is field help or coordination/admin

This model uses three simple staff-cost bands.

## Scenario A: modest support

Per staff member:

- `€400–€600 / month`

Two staff:

- `€800–€1,200 / month`

Use this when:

- support is light
- hours are limited
- the business is still early

## Scenario B: stronger support

Per staff member:

- `€600–€900 / month`

Two staff:

- `€1,200–€1,800 / month`

Use this when:

- support is meaningful
- workload is rising
- the business is moving beyond solo mode

## Scenario C: heavy support

Per staff member:

- `€800–€1,200 / month`

Two staff:

- `€1,600–€2,400 / month`

Use this when:

- roles are closer to full-time
- service complexity is higher
- the business is operating at a more mature level

## 6. Monthly scenario examples

## Scenario 1: 10 clients, solo mode

Assume:

- 10 clients
- mid pricing: `€180`
- no staff yet
- operating costs: `€700`

### Revenue

- `10 x 180 = €1,800`

### Costs

- staff: `€0`
- other costs: `€700`

### Remaining before tax / founder draw

- `€1,100`

### Meaning

This is a small early-stage business.

It is not yet a strong income machine, but it can support proof of concept.

---

## 7. Founder interpretation rule

The money remaining after staff and operating costs is not “free profit.”

It still has to support:

- founder income
- taxes
- cash buffer
- growth costs
- unexpected problems

So a leftover number should be read as:

- business capacity

not:

- founder personal income only

---

## 8. Pricing lesson

The biggest lesson from this model is simple:

### If pricing is too low

STREHË may become:

- busy
- stressed
- operationally heavy

without becoming financially strong

### If pricing is healthier

STREHË has a better chance to become:

- sustainable
- staffable
- calmer
- investable

This is why underpricing is one of the biggest risks.

---

## 9. Staffing lesson

Staffing must happen only when:

- workload is proven
- process is repeatable
- revenue can support it

### First likely staffing ladder

1. founder only
2. one field support person
3. second support or coordination help
4. broader team later

The business should not rush to two staff too early unless demand truly requires it.

---

## 10. Best-case Year 1 financial shape

For Year 1, a healthy target is not necessarily 30 clients immediately.

A healthier Year 1 success could be:

- 5-8 active apartments by New Year
- a clear and repeatable service
- early revenue proof
- no destructive overload

30 clients is possible later, but it may come too early for a calm solo launch if the system is not ready.

---

## 11. 30-client warning

If 30 clients arrive too early:

- do not activate all of them at once
- phase onboarding
- simplify initial service where needed
- protect quality
- hire only when the bottleneck is clear

Rapid demand is good news, but unmanaged demand can damage reputation fast.

---

## 12. Working summary

The model suggests:

- 30 clients can become a real business
- 30 clients with 2 staff can work
- but only if pricing is not too low

The clearest financial conclusion is:

`STREHË should avoid cheap pricing if it wants to support staff, keep quality high, and build a healthy business.`
