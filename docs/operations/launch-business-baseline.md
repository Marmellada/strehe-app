# STREHE Launch Business Baseline

## Purpose

This is the lean v1 business baseline. It turns the pricing and cost drafts into the working launch decision without adding extra paperwork.

## 1. Launch Package Structure

Use three packages for launch planning:

| Package | Internal launch price | Visits included | Launch role |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Essential Check | `€69/month` | 1 visit/month | Entry package for basic reassurance |
| Care Plus | `€109/month` | 2 visits/month | Main package to sell first |
| Arrival Ready | `€159/month` | 2 visits/month + readiness support | Higher-touch package for return visits and preparation |

These are working launch prices, not permanent public prices. They can change after real client feedback.

---

## 2. Included Visit Counts

Launch visit rhythm:

- Essential Check: 1 scheduled visit per month
- Care Plus: 2 scheduled visits per month
- Arrival Ready: 2 scheduled visits per month, plus arrival-readiness coordination

Weekly recurring service is not part of the default launch offer. It can be sold later only if workload and pricing support it.

---

## 3. Add-On Services

Allowed v1 add-ons:

| Add-on | Working price range | Rule |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Extra visit | `€25-€35` | For one-off checks outside package rhythm |
| Pre-arrival readiness visit | `€35-€50` | If not included in package |
| Technician coordination | `€20-€40` per issue | Does not include technician cost |
| Deep cleaning | Quote / partner cost | Client pays separately |

Not included by default:

- repair costs
- parts/materials
- cleaning
- renovations
- unlimited errands
- unlimited WhatsApp coordination

---

## 4. First-Year Pricing Model

Working target:

- sell Care Plus as the main package
- keep Essential Check limited so the business does not become too cheap for the workload
- use Arrival Ready carefully so it does not become unlimited concierge work
- charge extra visits and technician coordination separately

The Year 1 goal is not maximum client count. The goal is a manageable client mix with enough average monthly value to pay for time, fuel, admin, and risk.

---

## 5. Solo Operating Limit

Founder-only operation is the default launch mode.

Practical solo limit:

- comfortable learning phase: 0-8 clients
- possible but busy: 8-15 clients
- start planning help: 12-15 clients
- likely needs reliable part-time help: 15-20 clients

If clients are mostly Care Plus or Arrival Ready, help may be needed earlier because visit frequency and communication load are higher.

---

## 6. Staff Trigger Points

Do not hire fixed full-time staff before revenue proves the workload.

Use this trigger model:

| Stage | Staffing decision |
| --- | --- |
| 0-8 clients | Founder only |
| 8-15 clients | Occasional contractor or part-time field help if visits bunch together |
| 12-20 clients | One part-time field helper becomes realistic |
| 15-25 clients | Part-time operations/admin help may be needed |
| 25-35 clients | Consider field ops plus part-time coordinator only if margins support it |

---

## 7. First Hire Decision

Workers are not required on day one.

First hire type:

- part-time field helper or trusted contractor
- paid per visit, per half-day, or flexible monthly arrangement
- focused on visits, photos, key discipline, and simple reporting

Working wage range:

- early part-time helper: `€300-€500/month`
- stronger part-time operations support: `€500-€700/month`
- avoid fixed two-person payroll until average client value and client count support it

---

## 8. Operating Cost Assumptions

Use these working assumptions:

- early solo non-staff monthly costs: around `€500-€800`
- around 30 clients, non-staff monthly costs: around `€1,200-€1,800`
- repairs, parts, cleaning, technician fees, and large errands are client-paid or quoted separately
- no office/micro-base unless it clearly improves trust or operations enough to justify the cost
- key cabinet, labels, transport, phone, hosting, and basic admin are core launch costs

---

## 9. Launch Decision Summary

For launch:

- packages are finalized enough to configure and sell manually
- public pricing can still be tested before publishing everywhere
- workers are later, not day-one
- the first hire should be flexible field help
- solo operation stays the default until real workload proves otherwise
