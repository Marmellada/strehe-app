# Strehe-Prona Business Plan

## Version Focus

This business plan is built around the real niche Strehe-Prona intends to serve:

- Diaspora apartment owners
- Prishtina and nearby areas
- With Fushë Kosovë treated as an important adjacent micro-market

This is not a generic maintenance company and not a software startup. Strehe-Prona is a property oversight and owner-support business. The software is an internal operating tool, not the product sold to the customer.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Strehe-Prona is a Prishtina-based property oversight service designed for diaspora apartment owners who cannot monitor, maintain, or coordinate their properties day to day.

The company solves a very specific problem: many diaspora owners hold apartments in Kosovo as long-term family assets, second homes, future-use homes, or investment properties, but they lack a trusted local operator who can regularly check the apartment, identify problems early, prepare it before arrival, coordinate minor fixes, and manage practical issues such as keys, utilities, and third-party work.

Strehe-Prona aims to become that trusted local operator.

The business will launch with recurring monthly care packages and a set of higher-value add-on services:

- Monthly apartment checks
- Condition reporting with photos and short videos
- Ventilation and basic preventive checks
- Utility and bill handling
- Arrival preparation before the owner returns
- Small fixes
- Renovation oversight and project supervision
- Coordination of external contractors when needed

The company will initially serve apartments only. Houses introduce a different operational profile and will be considered after the first 6 to 12 months.

The launch radius is:

- Prishtina
- Nearby areas
- Especially Fushë Kosovë, where the business sees strong relevance due to the large number of vacant or intermittently used apartments

The company’s competitive advantage comes from combining:

- Trust-based local presence
- A recurring service model
- Clear reporting standards
- Secure key and document handling
- A low-overhead founder-led operating model
- An internal software platform for contracts, tasks, reporting, keys, billing, and client operations

Strehe-Prona’s aim is not to compete on cheap labor. Its aim is to sell peace of mind, control, visibility, and local representation to owners who are not present.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

Diaspora apartment owners often face the same recurring frustrations:

- They cannot physically inspect the apartment regularly
- They depend on relatives, neighbors, or ad hoc favors
- Small issues are discovered too late
- There is no standard reporting
- Keys and access are handled informally
- Utilities and bills can be missed or mismanaged
- Before arriving in Kosovo, the apartment may not be ready
- During small renovation or repair works, they lack a reliable local point of control

Most informal alternatives solve only fragments of this problem:

- A cleaner is not a property overseer
- A builder is not a trusted monthly checker
- A relative is not a scalable, accountable operator
- A real estate agency often does not want this level of recurring operational care

Strehe-Prona exists to fill that gap.

## 4. Value Proposition

Strehe-Prona offers diaspora owners a simple promise:

`Your apartment is not left unattended. You have a trusted local operator who checks it, documents it, responds early, and coordinates what needs to be done.`

This value proposition has five pillars:

- Oversight: the apartment is checked regularly
- Prevention: issues are found before they become expensive
- Visibility: every visit is documented
- Coordination: the owner does not need to arrange everything personally
- Peace of mind: there is one trusted point of contact

## 5. Services and Pricing Architecture

## 5.1 Monthly Packages

### Essential - EUR 29 / month / property

Includes:

- 1 visit per month
- Basic apartment condition check
- Ventilation
- Check of water, electricity, windows, and door
- 5 to 10 photos plus 1 short video
- Report by WhatsApp or email

Best for:

- Owners who want simple periodic control and peace of mind

### Care - EUR 49 / month / property

Includes:

- 2 visits per month
- Photo and video report
- Up to 30 minutes of small monthly work included
- Immediate notification if a problem is found
- Higher intervention priority

Examples of included small work:

- Replacing a bulb
- Basic reset
- Small simple fix

Best for:

- Owners who want stronger monitoring plus small practical intervention

### Premium - EUR 79 / month / property

Includes:

- Up to 4 visits per month
- Detailed report
- Priority in every intervention
- Coordination with workers or suppliers
- Discount on extra services
- Option to include 1 Arrival Ready service per year as a promotion or bundled benefit

Best for:

- Owners who want serious ongoing oversight, not only occasional checks

## 5.2 Add-On Services

### A. Arrival Ready Service

#### Arrival Ready A - EUR 50 to 60

Includes:

- Ventilation
- Basic control before arrival
- Check of batteries, lights, AC functionality, and general readiness

#### Arrival Ready B - EUR 150

Includes:

- All of A
- Vacuuming
- Dust cleaning
- Toilet cleaning

#### Arrival Ready C - EUR 250 to 300

Includes:

- All of B
- Dish washing
- Curtain washing
- Carpet washing
- General deeper cleaning

Business value:

- Emotionally strong service
- High trust-building potential
- Natural fit for diaspora owners
- Strong cross-sell from monthly packages

### B. Renovation Oversight / Project Supervision

Positioning:

- Strehe-Prona does not need to be the renovator
- Strehe-Prona acts as coordinator, local supervisor, and quality/checkpoint reporter

Role:

- Single point of contact for the owner
- Photo/video progress reporting
- Site visit coordination
- Basic quality visibility
- Contractor coordination and communication

Pricing approach:

- Small project fixed fee: EUR 150 to 300
- Larger project management fee: 10% to 15%

Rationale:

- Small projects are not worth percentage-only pricing
- Larger projects justify management-fee logic

### C. Bill and Utility Management

Positioning:

- Optional monthly add-on
- Used when owner wants support, not forced every month

Possible customer flow:

- Owner requests it when needed
- Strehe-Prona collects, reminds, pays with authorization, and charges back the expense plus service fee

Suggested pricing direction:

- EUR 9 to 15 per month as an add-on
- Or included partly in Premium later if strategically useful

Business value:

- High stickiness
- Strong trust signal
- Makes the relationship harder to replace

## 6. Standard Visit Protocol

Every visit should follow a fixed reporting standard.

Minimum standard:

- Photo of entrance
- Photo of bathroom
- Photo of kitchen
- Photo of windows
- Photo of utility meters where relevant
- Short video
- Fixed checklist
- Date and time of visit
- Short conclusion:
  - Condition OK
  - Issue found
  - Action needed

Why this matters:

- Creates consistency
- Builds professional trust
- Makes service feel structured and premium
- Differentiates Strehe-Prona from informal help

## 7. Market Thesis

The business thesis rests on a strong directional market logic:

- Kosovo’s diaspora remains financially important
- Real estate is a major destination of diaspora-linked money
- Kosovo has a large stock of uninhabited homes
- Prishtina has the largest number of uninhabited homes nationally
- Fushë Kosovë also has a large housing stock and is highly relevant to this business model

These points do not prove exact diaspora ownership share for all apartments in the target radius. However, together they strongly support the existence of a meaningful addressable market for diaspora-focused property oversight.

### What Is Verified

- The Central Bank of Kosovo reported EUR 363.4 million in remittances in Q2 2025.
- The same CBK report stated that 80.8% of FDI in Q2 2025 was concentrated in real estate, driven largely by diaspora demand and rising domestic real estate prices.
- ASK-based reporting stated Kosovo had around 182,849 uninhabited homes in the 2024 census.
- ASK-based reporting also stated Prishtina had 88,530 apartments and houses, while Fushë Kosovë had 27,707.

### What Is Not Treated As Verified

The statement that more than 60% of new apartments are owned by diaspora has not been confirmed with a strong official source in this plan. It may be valid as market intelligence from builders and operators, but for now it should be treated as founder insight, not a documented fact.

## 19B. Staffing Thresholds and Hiring Triggers

This section is especially important because the business cannot rely on vague optimism about “handling more clients somehow.”

The plan should define when new staff becomes necessary.

### Launch Stage: 0 to 35 Active Clients

Suggested structure:

- 2 founders active
- finance/admin support
- contractor network

At this stage:

- founders can perform most visits
- reporting can remain founder-controlled
- service quality can be tightly supervised
- route density is still developing

Main priority:

- trust building
- customer feedback
- process refinement

### Early Growth Stage: 35 to 80 Active Clients

Suggested structure:

- 2 founders active
- finance/admin support
- contractor network
- stronger process discipline

At this stage:

- founder workload becomes heavier
- visit frequency starts pressuring reporting time
- customer communication becomes more frequent

Main trigger to watch:

- if reporting quality starts slipping
- if response speed drops
- if one founder is trapped in operational overload

Action:

- formalize templates
- tighten visit routes
- prepare for first field support hire

### Scale Trigger 1: Around 80 to 140 Active Clients

Suggested additional hire:

- 1 field operations assistant or property visit officer

Why this matters:

- At around this level, founders should not try to personally handle every visit forever
- the company needs relief in repetitive field execution

Expected shift:

- founders move more toward quality control, client coordination, and oversight
- first field support handles part of the visit load

### Scale Trigger 2: Around 140 to 220 Active Clients

Suggested team:

- founders
- 1 field operations assistant
- 1 second field support / inspector
- finance/admin support

At this level:

- scheduling becomes more complex
- route planning matters more
- owner communication volume increases
- quality consistency becomes harder without clear controls

Expected operational change:

- stronger daily planning
- role separation between field execution and coordination

### Scale Trigger 3: Around 220 to 350 Active Clients

Suggested team:

- founders
- 2 field staff
- 1 admin / customer coordination support
- finance/admin support
- contractor network

At this level:

- a real service operations structure begins to form
- one founder should spend less time doing routine visits and more time managing quality, escalations, and partner coordination

Main priority:

- consistency
- service reliability
- customer retention

### Scale Trigger 4: 350+ Active Clients

Suggested structure:

- founders
- multiple field staff
- dedicated admin / coordination support
- stronger scheduling and route systems
- more formal contractor supervision

At this stage:

- the company becomes an actual operations business
- training and management discipline matter as much as sales

### Strategic Staffing Principle

Do not hire because growth sounds exciting.

Hire when one of these is true:

- visit quality begins to drop
- reports become delayed
- founders are losing time for sales and quality control
- response time becomes too slow
- route density creates enough recurring field work to justify a role

This principle reduces the risk of over-hiring too early.

## 19D. Surprise Reduction Framework

The user concern about surprises is valid. This business should therefore use trigger-based planning.

Every month, management should monitor:

- active clients
- visit volume
- package mix
- response speed
- report completion speed
- add-on revenue share
- contractor issue rate
- customer complaints
- referral rate

If 2 or more of these begin to worsen at the same time, the business should pause growth pressure and fix operations before accelerating again.

This is important because trust-sensitive businesses are damaged more by inconsistency than by slower growth.

## 19H. Service SOP Appendix

The company should not depend on memory. It should depend on repeatable operating procedures.

### SOP 1: Standard Monthly Visit

Steps:

1. Confirm route and access
2. Enter property and record timestamp
3. Follow fixed checklist
4. Take required photos
5. Record short video
6. Note any issue found
7. Send report
8. Log visit in the internal system

### SOP 2: Issue Escalation

If issue found:

1. classify urgency
2. notify client
3. propose action
4. assign internal or external resolution path
5. document outcome

### SOP 3: Arrival Ready Flow

1. confirm owner arrival date
2. confirm service level A, B, or C
3. schedule cleaning/prep work
4. verify utilities/basic readiness
5. do final check
6. send completion report before arrival

### SOP 4: Bill and Utility Handling

1. collect bill data
2. confirm owner authorization
3. pay or coordinate payment
4. send proof to owner
5. record service fee and reimbursable expense

### SOP 5: Renovation Oversight

1. define project scope
2. define pricing format: fixed fee or percentage
3. agree reporting frequency
4. conduct milestone checks
5. send photo/video reports
6. document issues and final status

### Why SOPs Matter

- They reduce surprises
- They make training easier
- They make service quality more repeatable
- They lower the risk of reputation damage
